League History

The Liberian First Division is the top professional football league in Liberia, founded in 1956 and run by the Liberia Football Association. Famous clubs like Invincible Eleven, Mighty Barrolle, and LISCR FC have shaped the league's history. Liberia has produced international stars including George Weah, Africa’s only Ballon d’Or winner.

The league uses modern stadiums, youth development, and competitive football. Top teams qualify for CAF competitions such as the CAF Champions League and CAF Confederation Cup.
